WHY RADIUS OF HOLLOW MATTERS FOR YOUR GAME

Choosing the right radius of hollow (ROH) is one of the most critical decisions a hockey player can make. It's the difference between having the "bite" you need to turn on a dime and the "glide" you need to beat a defender in a footrace.

What is Radius of Hollow?

ROH refers to the concave curve ground into the bottom of your skate blade. A smaller radius (like 3/8") creates a deeper groove, providing more bite but less glide. A larger radius (like 5/8" or 1") creates a shallower groove, allowing for more speed but less grip in the turns.

Pro Tip:

"Heavier players often benefit from a larger radius because their weight already provides natural bite. Lighter players might need a smaller radius to help them dig into the ice."

Which one is for you?

  • 3/8" - 1/2": Best for aggressive skaters, forwards who turn quickly, and lighter players.
  • 5/8" - 3/4": The "Goldilocks" zone for most intermediate players. Balance of speed and grip.
  • 1" +: Preferred by many pro defensemen and heavy-skating power forwards.
