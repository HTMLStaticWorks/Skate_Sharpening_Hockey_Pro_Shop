BEST OFF-ICE SUMMER DRILLS FOR ELITE HANDS

The best players aren't made in the winter—they're made in the summer. When the rinks are closed and the sun is out, the elite-level players are in their driveways, working on the fundamentals that win games.

The 'Figure 8' Drill

Place two pucks or markers about three feet apart. Using a stick-handling ball or a Green Biscuit, maneuver the puck in a figure-8 pattern around the markers. Focus on keeping your head up and using your wrists to roll the puck, rather than your whole arms.

Pro Tip:

"Train like you play. Wear your gloves during off-ice stick-handling drills to ensure the 'feel' is consistent with what you'll experience during a game."

Reactionary stick-handling

Have a partner stand across from you and call out colors or directions. As they call them, move the puck to that specific area as fast as possible. This trains your brain to react to game-like situations where you don't have time to think about your hands.
